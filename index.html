One of the most adventurous trips I can imagine is an expedition to the North Pole. To travel to this remote polar region would be an incredible opportunity to experience immense glaciers, arctic wildlife like polar bears and seals, and the phenomenon of 24-hour daylight during summer months when the sun never fully sets. Trekking across the frozen Arctic Ocean along with a knowledgeable guide, braving sub-zero temperatures and ice-covered landscapes, would be the thrill of a lifetime. An excursion by ship or smaller zodiac boats could provide chances to encounter native polar bear populations and take in enormous icebergs carved by the sea. Seeing the dazzling Northern Lights dance across the night sky against a backdrop of glittering snow is high on my bucket list too. Shiraz

Shiraz is one of the destinations that you will definitely want to visit when travelling to Iran. With Persepolis, Pasargadae, Hafez Tomb, Saadi Tomb and so many other historical and cultural attractions, we can call Shiraz the beauty of Persian culture and history.

Isfahan

In terms of historical monuments and tourism attractions, Isfahan is one of the major cities of Iran. This city is called "half of the world" by its people as they believe it has the beauties of one half of a whole world in itself.

Tehran

Tehran is home to many historical sites, including the royal complexes of Golestan, Sa'dabad, and Niavaran, where the last two dynasties of the former Imperial State of Iran were seated.
